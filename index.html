Love Letter

To My Dearest Love, on Your 17th Birthday ♡

Happy 17th Birthday, my love.

I've been staring at this blank page for far longer than I want to admit because every time I try to write about you, I find myself struggling to put into words what my heart seems to understand so easily. How do you explain what someone means to you when they've become such a natural part of your life? How do you describe the person who quietly became your favorite thought, your safest place, and the best part of so many ordinary days?

Seventeen years ago, the world welcomed you into it. It was just another day to everyone else, but now I can't help thinking about how lucky the world was that day. Because somewhere, a boy was born who would grow into someone capable of making people feel loved simply by being himself. And somehow, years later, I got lucky enough to know that person.

I don't tell you this enough, but sometimes I stop and think about how much you've changed my life. Not in some dramatic, movie-like way. You changed it quietly. Gently. The way sunlight slowly fills a room without anyone noticing. Before you, I never realized how guarded I was. I never realized how much of myself I kept hidden from the world. Then you came along, and somehow being myself felt easy. Being vulnerable felt less frightening. You made me softer without even trying, and I don't think I ever want to be the person I was before I met you.

What I love most about you isn't just the way you make me happy. It's the way you exist in my life without even trying and somehow make everything feel lighter. You're in the songs I listen to, in the random moments that make me smile, in every exciting thing that happens that makes me immediately think, "I can't wait to tell him." You've become part of the little things, and I think that's how you know someone truly matters. Not when they're only present in your biggest moments, but when they quietly live in all the small ones too.

Sometimes it's honestly a little scary to admit how much you mean to me. Not because I regret it, but because caring this deeply about someone makes your heart feel exposed. There are moments when I look at you and feel overwhelmed by how important you've become to me. You are no longer just someone I love. You've become someone whose happiness matters to me, whose dreams I find myself rooting for, whose sadness I wish I could take away, and whose smile feels like one of my favorite things in the world.

As you turn seventeen today, I hope you know how loved you are. I hope you know that the people around you are better because you're here. I hope you never lose the kindness in your heart, the dreams you carry, or the things that make you uniquely you. And if there ever comes a day when you doubt yourself, I hope you remember that there is someone who sees so much beauty in you, even in the parts you struggle to love yourself.

Thank you for every conversation, every laugh, every memory, and every moment you've shared with me. Thank you for becoming such an important part of my life. Meeting you wasn't just one of the happiest things that happened to me. It changed me. It taught me that love can feel safe, gentle, and real all at once.

I don't know what the future holds for us. Nobody does. But what I do know is that if I could go back and live this life all over again, I would still want every road to lead me to you. I would still choose every conversation, every memory, every moment that brought me closer to the person writing themselves into my heart without even realizing it.

Happy Birthday, my love.

If loving you is the one thing I get right in this life, then I think that's more than enough for me. No matter how many birthdays come and go, no matter how much time passes, I will always be grateful that seventeen years ago, the world gave me the chance to one day know you.

Forever cheering for you.
Forever grateful for you.
Forever loving you. ♡
